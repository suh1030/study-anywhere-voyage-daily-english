Part 1 — Daily Routine
Jamie
Hey Mira! I've been meaning to ask you this for a while — what does a typical day actually look like for you?
嘿，Mira！我想問你這個問題很久了——你的日常到底是什麼樣子？
I've been meaning to我一直想要（做某事）
Mira
Ha, good question. Honestly, it's pretty structured. I live about an hour outside the city, but I work downtown. So just the geography alone means my day has to start really early.
哈，好問題。老實說蠻規律的。我住在離市區約一小時的地方，但在市中心上班，光是距離就意味著我的一天必須很早開始。
coastal海岸的geography地理位置
Jamie
Wow, that's quite a distance. How long does the commute take?
哇，距離還蠻遠的。通勤要多久？
commute通勤
Mira
About an hour each way on a good day. Sometimes longer if the train is delayed or if I have to transfer. I take the train — it's the most reliable option for me.
順利的話單程約一小時，有時更長。我搭火車——真的沒有其他實際的選擇。
transfer轉乘realistic實際的、務實的
Jamie
An hour each way every single day — does it ever feel exhausting?
每天單程一小時——你有沒有覺得很累？
exhausting令人精疲力竭的
Mira
It did at first. The first few months were rough — I'd get home feeling completely drained. But I've genuinely gotten used to it. I use that time deliberately: podcasts in the morning, zone out on the way back. It's my decompression time.
剛開始確實是。前幾個月很辛苦——回到家都精力耗盡。但現在真的習慣了。我很刻意運用那段時間：早上聽 Podcast，回程放空。那是我的解壓時間。
drained精力耗盡的deliberately刻意地decompression解壓、放鬆緩衝
Jamie
What time do you usually wake up?
你通常幾點起床？
Mira
Around six, give or take. I need time to get ready without rushing. I try to arrive at the office by eight-thirty so I can ease into the day before things get busy.
大約六點，前後都有。我需要時間好好準備，不慌不忙。試著八點半前到辦公室，慢慢進入狀態再開始工作。
give or take大約、誤差範圍ease into慢慢進入狀態
Jamie
Are you naturally a morning person?
你天生是晨型人嗎？
morning person晨型人
Mira
Definitely not. I used to hit snooze multiple times and still feel groggy for the first hour. But commuting forces you into a rhythm whether you like it or not. After a while your body just adapts. Now I wake up before my alarm most days.
絕對不是。我以前會一直按貪睡鍵，整個早上頭一小時還是昏昏沉沉的。但通勤不管你喜不喜歡都會強迫你進入節奏。過一段時間身體就適應了。現在大多數日子鬧鐘響之前就醒了。
groggy昏昏沉沉的adapts適應
Part 2 — Work Life
Jamie
Let's talk about work. You work in tech — what does a typical day actually look like?
來聊聊工作。你在科技業——實際上一天是什麼樣子？
Mira
I build and maintain software systems — a mix of backend engineering and working with AI models. There's a lot of problem-solving involved, and no two days are quite the same.
我建構和維護軟體系統——結合後端工程與 AI 模型的應用。涉及很多問題解決，每天都不太一樣。
infrastructure基礎設施data pipelines資料管道
Jamie
Is every day different, or more repetitive?
每天都不一樣，還是比較重複性的？
repetitive重複性的
Mira
It varies a lot. Some days I'm deep in code — debugging or designing something from scratch. Other days are meeting-heavy: reviewing work, discussing requirements, sometimes interviewing candidates. I prefer the deep work days. When I get into a flow state, hours pass without me noticing.
其實變化很大。有些日子深陷在程式碼裡——debugging 或從頭設計。其他日子會議比較多。我更喜歡深度工作的日子。進入心流狀態時，幾個小時可以在沒注意到的情況下流逝。
flow state心流狀態from scratch從頭開始
Jamie
What about stress?
那壓力呢？
Mira
It can get overwhelming — especially when deadlines stack up, or a system breaks in production. There's also an invisible pressure from working in a fast-moving field. Things change rapidly and you always feel like there's something new you should be learning.
有時確實會讓人喘不過氣——尤其是截止日期堆積，或系統在生產環境崩潰的時候。在快速變動的領域工作也有一種無形的壓力。
stacking up堆積、累積in production在正式上線的系統中
Jamie
How do you manage that pressure?
你怎麼應對這些壓力？
Mira
I've learned to break things down. When everything feels urgent, I step back and ask: what is the one thing that needs to happen right now? That helps me prioritize instead of panicking. I also try not to catastrophize — most problems have a solution.
我學會了把事情拆解。當所有事情同時感覺很緊急時，我退一步問自己：現在真正需要發生的一件事是什麼？這幫助我優先排序而不是恐慌。
prioritize做優先排序catastrophize災難化思考
Jamie
Do you find it hard to switch off from work at home?
你回家後很難從工作模式切換出來嗎？
switch off切換、抽離
Mira
Sometimes. But the commute home helps — by the time I'm off the train, I've had an hour to let my brain shift gears. It acts like a buffer zone. Once I'm through the door, I make a deliberate transition: change clothes, make tea, decompress for a bit. Small rituals that signal work is done.
有時候。但回程通勤很有幫助——等下火車時，已經有一小時讓大腦自然換擋了。就像工作模式和居家模式之間的緩衝區。一進門，我做刻意的轉換：換衣服、泡茶、放鬆一下。這些小儀式在說：今天的工作結束了。
shift gears換擋、轉換模式buffer zone緩衝區rituals儀式、固定習慣
Part 3 — Hobbies
Jamie
Photography, diving, crystals — where do you want to start?
攝影、潛水、水晶——你想從哪裡開始？
Mira
Photography. I've always had an eye for composition — even before a proper camera, I'd notice light and framing in everyday scenes. I only committed to it properly when I got a proper mirrorless camera.
攝影。我一直對構圖有感覺——即使在有正式相機之前，我也會注意到光線和取景。直到買了一台真正的無反相機才認真投入。
composition構圖framing取景
Jamie
Why Fujifilm?
為什麼選富士？
Mira
The color science — built-in profiles that give images a film-like quality. There's a warmth and grain to them that other brands don't quite replicate. And it's compact enough that I actually bring it with me, which is the whole point.
色彩科學——內建設定，讓照片有底片般的質感。有一種其他品牌無法完全複製的溫暖感和顆粒感。而且夠輕巧，讓我真的會帶出門，這才是重點。
film simulations底片模擬mimic模仿
Jamie
Tell me about diving.
說說潛水。
Mira
I got my Open Water certification a couple of years ago and almost didn't go through with it — the pool sessions made me nervous. There are things underwater that feel counterintuitive, like flooding your mask on purpose and clearing it calmly. Your instinct is to panic, but you have to override that.
幾年前考到 OW 執照，差點沒堅持下去——泳池訓練讓我很緊張。水下有些事感覺違反直覺，比如故意讓面鏡進水然後冷靜清除。你的本能是恐慌，但你必須克服那個。
counterintuitive違反直覺的override壓制本能
Jamie
What made you stick with it?
是什麼讓你堅持下去的？
stick with it堅持下去
Mira
The first time I actually descended into open water — everything went quiet. You hear your own breathing, see fish going about their lives completely indifferent to you, and the light underwater is unlike anything on the surface. I didn't expect to enjoy it this much.
第一次真正下沉到開放水域——一切都安靜了。你可以聽到自己的呼吸，看到魚兒完全無視你，水下的光線跟水面上的完全不同。我沒想到我會這麼喜歡它。
descended下沉、下潛indifferent to無視、漠不關心
Jamie
And the crystals?
那水晶呢？
Mira
Purely aesthetic for me. What I love is the geometry: the way minerals form perfectly structured shapes, the variation in color and transparency. Each piece is essentially a natural sculpture.
對我來說純粹是美學上的。我喜歡的是幾何形狀：礦石以完美結構形成的方式、顏色和透明度的變化。每一塊本質上都是天然雕塑。
geometry幾何形狀transparency透明度
Part 4 — Weekends
Jamie
With such a demanding week, how do you spend your weekends?
有這麼忙碌的一週，你週末怎麼度過？
demanding要求高的
Mira
I've become much more protective of my weekends. I used to feel guilty if I wasn't being productive. But I've realized rest is part of the process — if I don't recharge properly, my work suffers anyway.
我對週末變得更有保護意識了。以前如果不夠有生產力會感到愧疚。但我意識到休息是過程的一部分——如果沒有好好充電，無論如何工作也會受影響。
protective of珍視、有保護意識recharge充電、恢復精力
Jamie
What does a good weekend look like?
一個好的週末是什麼樣子的？
Mira
It starts slow — no alarm if I can help it. A slow morning with coffee and a good book. Then I might go out with my camera or stay home and write. Writing is how I process the week — observations, small moments that stuck with me, sometimes creative fragments.
開始得很慢——如果可以的話不設鬧鐘。喝咖啡配一本好書的悠閒早晨。然後可能帶相機出去或待在家寫作。寫作是我消化這一週的方式——觀察、讓我印象深刻的小時刻，有時候是創意片段。
if I can help it如果能避免的話fragments片段
Part 5 — Wrap Up
Jamie
Your life seems pretty full. Do you ever feel like it's too much?
你的生活感覺很充實。你有沒有覺得太多了？
Mira
Sometimes I feel stretched thin. But the key is none of these things feel like obligations — they're all things I genuinely chose. Even the commute is a trade-off I made consciously for the job I wanted. When you choose something, it's easier to accept the hard parts.
有時候感覺被拉得很薄。但關鍵是這些事情都不感覺像義務——都是我真正選擇的。即使是通勤，也是我為了想要的工作而有意識做出的取捨。
stretched thin同時應付太多事trade-off取捨
Jamie
What's one thing you're still working on?
有什麼你還在努力的事情？
Mira
Speaking more naturally, for one. I can express ideas fine in writing, but saying things spontaneously without translating in my head first — that's where I still struggle. I want to reach a point where the language just flows, without the lag. That's partly why I started doing things like this.
更自然地表達，其中之一。我能用文字清楚表達想法，但即興說出來而不需要先在腦中翻譯——那是我仍然掙扎的地方。我想達到語言自然流暢的程度。這也是我開始做這類事情的部分原因。
the lag延遲、腦中翻譯的停頓
Jamie
For what it's worth, you're already doing it. Thanks so much for sharing all of this.
就目前而言，你已經在做了。非常感謝你分享這一切。
for what it's worth就目前而言
Mira
Thanks for having me. It was nice to talk through all of this out loud — in English, no less. See you in the next episode.
謝謝你邀請我。大聲說出這一切其實很不錯——而且還是用英文。下集見。
no less而且還（強調）
